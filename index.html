Running a 3D print business
is harder than it should be

Most makers start with passion — and end up buried in spreadsheets, lost orders, and no idea if they're actually making money.

😤
"I have no idea if I'm making money"
You sell prints every week but at the end of the month there's nothing left. You're guessing prices and wondering where the profit went.
📋
"My orders are in WhatsApp, notes, and Excel"
You take orders through 3 different channels, forget to follow up, and sometimes deliver late — not because you're disorganized, but because there's no single system.
🧾
"Invoices take forever and look amateur"
Each invoice is copy-pasted from the last one, manually edited in Word, and it still never looks quite right. Clients notice.
🧵
"I run out of filament mid-job"
You think you have enough PLA, start a 10-hour print, and realize halfway through the spool is almost empty. The order is late. The client is unhappy.
💸
"I sell on Etsy but nobody finds my listings"
You spend hours making the product, then 10 minutes writing the listing. The algorithm buries it. Your competitor with worse prints ranks above you.
🖨️
"Failed prints cost me more than I realize"
Stringing, layer shifts, warping — every failed print wastes filament, electricity and time. But you've never added it up. It's probably more than you think.
